Resources

A collection of courses, papers, and video explanations that I found useful while learning machine learning and AI. Organized from foundational to current, for anyone starting the same journey.

01. COURSES & MOOCs

Before diving into papers, build strong foundations. These courses cover the math, programming, and core ML concepts you'll need. All free or auditable.

Harvard / edX

CS50x: Introduction to Computer Science

The best starting point if you're new to CS. Covers C, Python, data structures, algorithms, and web. David Malan's lectures are unmatched.
MIT OpenCourseWare

6.006: Introduction to Algorithms

Rigorous algorithms course. Essential for understanding computational complexity and the data structures underlying ML systems.
MIT OpenCourseWare

18.06: Linear Algebra (Gilbert Strang)

The math behind everything in ML. Eigenvalues, SVD, projections, vector spaces. Strang makes it intuitive.
Coursera / Stanford

Machine Learning Specialization (Andrew Ng)

The updated version of the course that introduced millions to ML. Covers regression, neural nets, decision trees, recommender systems.
Coursera / deeplearning.ai

Deep Learning Specialization (Andrew Ng)

Five courses covering neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, sequence models, and practical deep learning. The natural follow-up to the ML Specialization.
MIT OpenCourseWare

6.036: Introduction to Machine Learning

MIT's undergrad ML course. More mathematical rigor than Ng's courses. Good for building theory alongside intuition.
edX / Harvard

Stat 110: Introduction to Probability

Joe Blitzstein's probability course. Essential for understanding Bayesian methods, generative models, and statistical ML.
Stanford / YouTube

CS231n: CNNs for Visual Recognition

Andrej Karpathy and Fei-Fei Li's legendary computer vision course. If you're doing anything with images, start here.
02. SEMINAL ML/AI PAPERS

33 papers that shaped the field, each paired with the best video explanation I could find. Ordered chronologically within eras — start from the top if you're building up, or filter by topic.

Filter:
Foundations · 1986 – 2006
01
Backpropagation 1986
Andrej Karpathy — The spelled-out intro to neural networks & backprop
Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams. The algorithm that made training deep nets tractable. Karpathy builds it from scratch in numpy.
ARCH
→
02
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) 1997
Video Explanation — LSTM Explained
Hochreiter & Schmidhuber. Solved the vanishing gradient problem in RNNs with gating mechanisms. Dominated sequence modeling for 20 years.
ARCHNLP
→
Deep Learning Revolution · 2012 – 2016
03
AlexNet — ImageNet Classification with Deep CNNs 2012
Yannic Kilcher — Classic paper series
Krizhevsky, Sutskever & Hinton. The paper that reignited deep learning. GPU-trained deep CNNs dominating ImageNet by a wide margin.
CVARCH
→
04
Word2Vec — Distributed Representations of Words 2013
Rasa — Word2Vec / embedding concepts explained
Mikolov et al. Showed that shallow neural nets on next-word prediction encode rich semantic structure (king − man + woman ≈ queen). The origin of all modern embeddings.
NLPARCH
→
05
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) 2014
Yannic Kilcher — GAN paper explained
Goodfellow et al. Minimax game between generator and discriminator. Opened the entire field of deep generative modeling.
GENARCH
→
06
Neural Machine Translation with Attention (Bahdanau) 2014
Video Explanation — Bahdanau attention explained
Bahdanau, Cho & Bengio. The paper that introduced attention — soft alignment for seq2seq translation. The ancestor of transformers.
NLPARCH
→
07
ResNet — Deep Residual Learning 2015
Yannic Kilcher — ResNet paper explained
He et al. Skip connections that let gradients flow across 100+ layers. Still the backbone of most vision models.
CVARCH
→
08
Playing Atari with Deep RL (DQN) 2015
Yannic Kilcher — DQN paper explained
Mnih et al. (DeepMind). Q-learning + CNNs + experience replay. First proof that RL can learn superhuman policies from raw pixels.
RL
→
Transformer Era · 2017 – 2020
09
Attention Is All You Need (Transformer) 2017
Yannic Kilcher — Attention Is All You Need explained
Vaswani et al. Replaced recurrence with multi-head self-attention. The architectural shift behind every modern LLM.
NLPARCH
→
10
Let's Build GPT from Scratch 2017/2020
Andrej Karpathy — GPT implementation walkthrough (~2hr)
Builds GPT line-by-line in PyTorch. The best existing explanation of autoregressive transformers in practice.
NLPARCH
→
11
BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers 2018
Yannic Kilcher — BERT paper explained
Devlin et al. Masked language modeling + bidirectional context. Established the pretrain-then-finetune paradigm in NLP.
NLP
→
12
GPT-3: Language Models are Few-Shot Learners 2020
Yannic Kilcher — GPT-3 paper explained
Brown et al. 175B parameters showing emergent few-shot capabilities from scale alone. Triggered the LLM race.
NLP
→
Vision + Generative Frontier · 2020 – 2022
13
ViT — An Image is Worth 16x16 Words 2020
Yannic Kilcher — ViT paper explained
Dosovitskiy et al. Transformer self-attention on image patches with no convolutions. Vision and language sharing the same architecture.
CVARCH
→
14
Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPM) 2020/2021
Yannic Kilcher — DDPM & Diffusion Models Beat GANs
Ho et al. Iterative denoising framework that outperformed GANs. Foundation of Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, and Sora.
GENARCH
→
15
CLIP — Learning Transferable Visual Models from Language 2021
Yannic Kilcher — CLIP paper explained
Radford et al. Contrastive pretraining on 400M image-text pairs. Zero-shot classification. Backbone behind DALL-E 2 and Stable Diffusion.
CVNLPGEN
→
16
DINO — Self-Supervised Vision Transformers 2021
Video Explanation — DINO paper explained
Caron et al. Student-teacher self-distillation without labels. Attention maps localize objects with zero supervision. De facto pretraining for vision encoders.
CVGEN
→
17
InstructGPT — Training LMs to Follow Instructions with RLHF 2022
AssemblyAI — How ChatGPT is Trained
Ouyang et al. SFT + reward model + PPO. The recipe that turned GPT-3 into ChatGPT.
NLPRL
→
Scaling · Efficiency · Reasoning · 2022 – 2025
18
Chinchilla — Compute-Optimal LLM Training 2022
Video Explanation — Chinchilla Explained
Hoffmann et al. Showed existing LLMs were undertrained. The 20 tokens/parameter rule reshaped how every lab trains models.
ARCHNLP
→
19
FlashAttention — Fast and Memory-Efficient Attention 2022
Video Explanation — FlashAttention explained
Dao et al. IO-aware attention fusing matmul + softmax into a single CUDA kernel. Now baked into PyTorch and every modern training stack.
ARCHNLP
→
20
LoRA — Low-Rank Adaptation of LLMs 2022
Umar Jamil — LoRA paper explained
Hu et al. Freeze weights, inject trainable rank-r matrices. 10,000x fewer trainable params. The dominant fine-tuning paradigm.
ARCHNLP
→
21
Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning 2022
Video Explanation — CoT paper explained
Wei et al. Few-shot examples with reasoning steps dramatically improve math and logic tasks. The seed behind o1 and all reasoning models.
NLP
→
22
LLaMA — Open and Efficient Foundation LMs 2023
Yannic Kilcher — LLaMA paper explained
Touvron et al. 13B model outperforms GPT-3 175B. Spawned the entire open-source LLM ecosystem.
NLP
→
23
Segment Anything Model (SAM) 2023
Video Explanation — SAM paper explained
Kirillov et al. Foundation model for promptable image segmentation. Generalizes to any object with zero fine-tuning.
CVGEN
→
24
Mamba — Linear-Time Sequence Modeling with Selective SSMs 2023
Yannic Kilcher — Mamba paper explained
Gu & Dao. State space model scaling linearly with sequence length. The most serious challenger to transformers for long sequences.
ARCHNLP
→
25
Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) 2023
AI Coffee Break — DPO paper explained
Rafailov et al. Collapses RLHF into a single supervised loss on preference pairs. Widely adopted by Llama 2, Mistral, and others.
NLPRL
→
26
DeepSeek-R1 — Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning 2025
Video Explanation — DeepSeek-R1 explained
Pure RL from a base LLM produces emergent chain-of-thought reasoning. Opened the test-time compute scaling era.
NLPRL
→
Adjacent Fields · Graphs · RL · Science AI · 2015 – 2021
27
GCN — Graph Convolutional Networks 2017
Aleksa Gordić — GCN paper explained
Kipf & Welling. The foundational GNN paper. Semi-supervised node classification with spectral graph convolutions.
GRAPH
→
28
GAT — Graph Attention Networks 2018
Aleksa Gordić — GAT paper explained
Veličković et al. Learned attention weights on neighbor aggregation. Self-attention applied to graph-structured data.
GRAPH

29
GraphSAGE — Inductive Representation Learning on Graphs 2017
Aleksa Gordić — GraphSAGE explained
Hamilton et al. Scalable, inductive GNNs. Sample neighborhoods, aggregate with learned functions. Foundation for production GNN systems.
GRAPH
→
30
PPO — Proximal Policy Optimization 2017

Schulman et al. Clipped surrogate objective for stable policy updates. OpenAI's default RL algorithm. Underlies RLHF / ChatGPT fine-tuning.
RL
→
31
AlphaGo Zero / AlphaZero 2017–2018
Two Minute Papers — AlphaZero explained
Silver et al. (DeepMind). Zero human knowledge, pure self-play with MCTS + deep RL. Superhuman at chess and Go within hours.
RL
→
32
SAC — Soft Actor-Critic 2018
Video Explanation — SAC explained
Haarnoja et al. Entropy-regularized RL for continuous control. Dominant off-policy algorithm for robotics.
RL
→
33
AlphaFold 2 — Protein Structure Prediction 2021
Yannic Kilcher — AlphaFold 2 explained
Jumper et al. (DeepMind). Solved the 50-year protein folding problem. 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
