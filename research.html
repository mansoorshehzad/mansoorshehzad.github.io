01. ACTIVE RESEARCH
Under Review · MICCAI 2026

Echo-SCAR: Temporal Feature Learning for Myocardial Scar Detection from Routine Echocardiography

The Question

Myocardial scar is a critical determinant of patient prognosis, guiding decisions about revascularization, device implantation, and medical management. The gold standard for detecting it — cardiac MRI with late gadolinium enhancement — costs $1,000–$3,000 per study and is unavailable in most clinical settings.

Echocardiography, on the other hand, is performed millions of times a year. It's cheap, fast, and everywhere. But it has historically been considered unable to characterize tissue at this level.

Can routine echocardiographic video — a test already being performed — detect myocardial scar without requiring cardiac MRI?

Why It Matters

If echo-based scar screening is feasible, it could enable opportunistic screening during routine exams — flagging high-risk patients for targeted CMR referral without any additional imaging, cost, or clinical workflow changes. A scalable diagnostic built on top of infrastructure that already exists.

Paper under review. Details available upon request.

IEEE ISBI 2025 · Poster Presentation

Automatic Video Segmentation of the Four Cardiac Chambers in Echocardiograms

Reliable segmentation is the foundation everything else is built on. This work developed automated, view-specific segmentation models for all four cardiac chambers from echocardiographic video, achieving Dice coefficients above 0.94 across views. These models form the backbone of the Echo-SCAR pipeline and enable scalable downstream analysis — from volume estimation to tissue characterization — without requiring manual clinical annotation.
