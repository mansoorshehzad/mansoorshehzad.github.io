01. WHO I AM

About

I'm a PhD student in Applied Physics at Rice University, developing computer vision and machine learning methods for cardiovascular imaging. My work at Houston Methodist Rice Digital Health Institute focuses on extracting diagnostic information from routine echocardiography — enabling non-invasive screening that currently requires expensive, specialized imaging like cardiac MRI.

I work closely with cardiologists and clinical data, which means my research is shaped by what's actually useful at the point of care, not just what's technically interesting.

02. CURRENT RESEARCH
Under Review · MICCAI 2026

Echo-SCAR

Can a routine echocardiogram — a test already being performed millions of times a year — detect myocardial scar without requiring an expensive cardiac MRI? We're investigating whether temporal features extracted from echo video contain enough hidden signal to screen for scar at the segment level.

#echocardiography #feature_learning #nnU-Net #cardiac_MRI
IEEE ISBI 2025 · Poster

Automatic Cardiac Chamber Segmentation

Automated segmentation of all four cardiac chambers from echocardiographic video. This work underpins the Echo-SCAR pipeline — reliable, view-specific segmentation is the foundation that makes downstream tissue characterization possible.

#segmentation #echocardiography #deep_learning
